About Me

Your Photo Here
charles.jpg

I’m a pharmacology researcher and PhD candidate at Northeastern University studying how membrane lipids shape ion channel function to drive smarter drug discovery. My work blends electrophysiology, molecular biology, and computational analysis, with expertise in patch clamp, cell culture, and site-directed mutagenesis.

I also develop custom Python-based tools, including a full data analysis GUI that streamlines electrophysiology and imaging workflows, freeing more time for experiments and speeding the path from data to discovery. My aim is to turn fundamental biophysics into real-world therapies, building the tools and knowledge that move science forward.

Research & Projects

Ion Channel Modulation

Investigating how membrane lipids modulate TMEM16A and NaV1.4 channels using patch-clamp electrophysiology. This work aims to understand fundamental mechanisms of ion channel regulation and identify new therapeutic targets for channelopathies and pain disorders.

Data Analysis Automation

Developed Python-based analysis pipelines that process large electrophysiological datasets, reducing manual analysis time from hours to minutes. These tools incorporate GUI interfaces for user-friendly operation and enhanced consistency of analysis parameters

Collaborative Studies

Providing targeted electrophysiology and molecular biology support for multi-lab projects on ion channels, membrane receptors, and transporters. Deliverables include high-impact datasets, publication-quality figures, and custom statistical analyses, with contributions spanning construct design, heterologous expression, high-throughput electrophysiology, toxicology screening, and data interpretation.
