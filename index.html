I am a pharmacology researcher investigating how membrane lipids modulate ion channel function to inform the rational design of novel therapeutics. Currently pursuing my PhD in Pharmacology at Northeastern University under the supervision of Dr. Leigh D. Plant.

My research combines cutting-edge electrophysiology techniques with molecular biology and computational analysis. I have five years of experience with patch-clamp electrophysiology, mammalian cell culture, and molecular techniques including site-directed mutagenesis.

I'm passionate about developing innovative approaches to drug discovery and have created Python analysis pipelines that greatly reduce data processing time for electrophysiology and imaging data.

Research & Projects

Ion Channel Modulation

Investigating how membrane lipids modulate TMEM16A and NaV1.4 channels using patch-clamp electrophysiology. This work aims to understand fundamental mechanisms of ion channel regulation and identify new therapeutic targets.

Data Analysis Automation

Developed Python-based analysis pipelines that process large electrophysiological datasets, reducing manual analysis time from hours to minutes. These tools incorporate GUI interfaces for user-friendly operation.

Collaborative Studies

Contributing electrophysiology and molecular biology expertise to multi-lab studies on ion channels and receptor pharmacology, with focus on bitter taste receptors and nicotinic receptors.
